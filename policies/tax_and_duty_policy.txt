TAX AND DUTY POLICY
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Policy Number: CS-POL-010
Version: 1.3

================================================================================
1. POLICY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

This policy governs sales tax, value-added tax (VAT), customs duties, and 
related tax and duty inquiries for domestic and international orders. It 
clarifies our responsibilities versus customer responsibilities and provides 
guidance for handling tax and duty-related customer questions.

================================================================================
2. DOMESTIC SALES TAX
================================================================================

2.1 Sales Tax Collection
    We collect sales tax on orders shipped to addresses in jurisdictions
    where we have tax nexus (physical presence or economic activity
    thresholds). Sales tax is calculated based on:
    - Shipping address (destination-based)
    - Product taxability in that jurisdiction
    - Current tax rates at time of purchase
    
    Sales tax is displayed at checkout before order completion.

2.2 Tax-Exempt Purchases
    Tax-exempt purchases may be available for:
    - Qualified resellers (with valid resale certificate)
    - Tax-exempt organizations (with valid exemption certificate)
    - Certain product categories exempt in specific jurisdictions
    
    Tax exemption requires:
    - Valid exemption certificate provided before purchase
    - Certificate verification and approval
    - Tax exemption applied to account or specific order

2.3 Sales Tax Refunds
    If sales tax incorrectly charged:
    - Refund tax amount if error confirmed
    - Process refund within 1-2 business days
    - Tax refund appears in 5-10 business days
    - If tax-exempt certificate provided post-purchase: Refund tax if
      certificate valid and retroactively applicable

2.4 Sales Tax Inquiries
    Common customer questions:
    - "Why was I charged tax?" - Explain nexus and tax requirements
    - "Can I get tax refunded?" - Only if error or valid exemption
    - "Tax amount seems wrong" - Verify calculation, explain if correct
    - "Tax not shown at checkout" - Tax calculated at checkout, may vary
      by shipping address

================================================================================
3. INTERNATIONAL VALUE-ADDED TAX (VAT)
================================================================================

3.1 VAT Collection
    For orders shipped to VAT-applicable regions (EU, UK, etc.):
    - VAT collected at checkout where we are required to collect
    - VAT rate based on destination country
    - VAT-inclusive pricing displayed where applicable
    - VAT amount shown separately on order confirmation

3.2 VAT-Exempt Purchases
    VAT exemptions may apply for:
    - Business customers with valid VAT number (B2B transactions)
    - Certain product categories
    - Specific country exemptions
    
    VAT exemption requires:
    - Valid VAT registration number
    - Business verification
    - Exemption applied before order completion

3.3 VAT Refunds
    VAT refunds available if:
    - VAT incorrectly charged due to our error
    - Valid VAT exemption provided post-purchase (if retroactively
      applicable)
    - Order cancelled or returned (VAT refunded with order refund)
    
    VAT refunds process with order refunds (5-10 business days).

================================================================================
4. CUSTOMS DUTIES AND FEES
================================================================================

4.1 Customer Responsibility
    For international orders, customer is responsible for:
    - All customs duties and import taxes
    - Customs clearance fees
    - Any additional fees imposed by destination country customs
    - Providing required customs documentation
    
    We are NOT responsible for customs duties or fees.

4.2 Duty Estimation
    - Estimated duties may be calculated at checkout (where available)
    - Estimates are approximate, final amount determined by customs
    - Actual duties may differ from estimate
    - Customer responsible for actual duty amount, not estimate

4.3 Duty Payment
    - Duties typically paid to carrier or customs upon delivery
    - Payment methods vary by country and carrier
    - Customer must pay duties to receive package
    - If duties not paid, package may be returned to sender

4.4 Duty Disputes
    If customer disputes duty charges:
    - Duties are set by destination country customs, not us
    - We cannot control or refund customs duties
    - Customer should contact carrier or customs authority
    - We can provide order documentation to assist customer

================================================================================
5. DUTY-FREE ORDERS
================================================================================

5.1 Duty-Free Thresholds
    Some countries have duty-free thresholds:
    - Orders below threshold: No duties (may still have taxes)
    - Orders above threshold: Duties apply
    - Thresholds vary by country and change periodically
    - Thresholds apply to order value, not individual items

5.2 Duty-Free Information
    - Duty-free thresholds displayed at checkout where available
    - Customer responsible for verifying current thresholds
    - Thresholds subject to change without notice
    - We provide estimates but cannot guarantee duty-free status

================================================================================
6. TAX AND DUTY CALCULATION ERRORS
================================================================================

6.1 Our Calculation Errors
    If we incorrectly calculate tax or duty:
    - Refund overcharge immediately
    - Correct calculation for future orders
    - Apologize for error
    - Document for system correction

6.2 Customer Calculation Disputes
    If customer believes calculation incorrect:
    - Verify calculation method and rates
    - Explain calculation if correct
    - If error confirmed: Correct and refund
    - Provide tax/duty calculation breakdown if helpful

6.3 System Errors
    If system error causes tax/duty issue:
    - Correct immediately
    - Refund any overcharges
    - Document error for technical team
    - Monitor for pattern of errors

================================================================================
7. TAX DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

7.1 Tax Invoices and Receipts
    - Order confirmations include tax breakdown
    - Tax invoices available in customer account
    - Tax invoices show tax amount, rate, and jurisdiction
    - Invoices suitable for business expense documentation

7.2 International Tax Documentation
    - Customs invoices provided with international shipments
    - Documentation includes order value and contents
    - Required for customs clearance
    - Additional documentation available upon request

7.3 Tax Certificate Requests
    - Tax exemption certificates: Submit before purchase for approval
    - Resale certificates: Submit for business account setup
    - Tax documentation: Available in account or upon request

================================================================================
8. REGIONAL TAX VARIATIONS
================================================================================

8.1 United States
    - Sales tax varies by state, county, city
    - Tax collected where we have nexus
    - Tax-exempt purchases with valid certificates
    - Tax displayed at checkout

8.2 Canada
    - GST/HST collected based on province
    - Tax rates vary by province
    - Business customers may be tax-exempt with valid number

8.3 European Union
    - VAT collected at destination country rate
    - VAT-inclusive pricing displayed
    - B2B transactions may be VAT-exempt with valid VAT number
    - VAT shown separately on invoices

8.4 United Kingdom
    - VAT collected at UK rate
    - Post-Brexit customs duties may apply
    - VAT and duties displayed separately where applicable

8.5 Australia
    - GST collected on orders over threshold
    - GST rate: 10%
    - Business customers may be GST-exempt

================================================================================
9. TAX-RELATED REFUNDS AND ADJUSTMENTS
================================================================================

9.1 Order Cancellations
    - Tax refunded with order cancellation
    - Tax refund processes with order refund (5-10 business days)
    - Full tax amount refunded for cancelled orders

9.2 Returns and Refunds
    - Tax refunded with order refund
    - Tax refund processes automatically with return refund
    - Partial returns: Tax refunded proportionally

9.3 Price Adjustments
    - Tax recalculated if order price adjusted
    - Tax difference refunded or charged as applicable
    - Tax adjustment processes with price adjustment

================================================================================
10. TAX EXEMPTION PROCESS
================================================================================

10.1 Exemption Application
    To apply for tax exemption:
    - Submit valid exemption certificate
    - Provide business information if applicable
    - Wait for verification and approval
    - Exemption applied to account or specific order

10.2 Exemption Verification
    - Certificates verified for validity and applicability
    - Business information verified for business exemptions
    - Verification typically 2-3 business days
    - Customer notified of approval or denial

10.3 Exemption Maintenance
    - Exemptions may expire and require renewal
    - Customer notified before exemption expiration
    - Update exemption certificates as needed
    - Exemptions subject to periodic review

================================================================================
11. ESCALATION CRITERIA
================================================================================

Escalate tax and duty issues to supervisor or specialist for:
- Complex tax exemption requests
- High-value tax disputes
- International duty disputes requiring documentation
- Tax calculation errors affecting multiple customers
- Regional tax law questions
- Business tax exemption verifications
- Customs duty disputes requiring carrier coordination
- Tax refund requests outside standard policy

================================================================================
12. COMMUNICATION GUIDELINES
================================================================================

When communicating about taxes and duties:
- Clearly explain tax/duty responsibility (ours vs. customer's)
- Provide tax/duty calculation breakdown when helpful
- Explain why taxes/duties apply or don't apply
- Set expectations for refund processing timelines
- Provide documentation or resources when available
- Show empathy for tax/duty frustrations (especially international)

================================================================================
13. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

All tax and duty interactions must include:
- Order number and tax/duty amounts
- Tax/duty calculation method or explanation
- Refund or adjustment details if applicable
- Exemption certificate information if discussed
- Regional tax information referenced
- Resolution actions taken
